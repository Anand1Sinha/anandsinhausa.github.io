WHY A4H ENDORSES

Scott Wiener

for U.S. House of Representatives (CA-11)

A4H is proud to support Scott Wiener for Congress in California's 11th District. As an experienced public servant, policy leader, and advocate for innovation, housing, public safety, and civil rights, Scott brings a pragmatic, results-oriented approach to representing one of America's most dynamic and diverse districts.

⚖️

Experienced Public Service Leadership

Scott has built a strong record of public service in California, with deep experience in lawmaking, coalition-building, and addressing complex challenges facing urban communities, families, and small businesses.

🏡

Housing, Affordability & Quality of Life

Scott has been a leading voice on housing affordability, infrastructure, and quality-of-life issues — priorities that matter deeply to working families, professionals, students, and seniors across the Bay Area.

💡

Innovation, Technology & Economic Opportunity

Representing San Francisco and one of the world's most important innovation regions, Scott understands the importance of technology, entrepreneurship, economic competitiveness, and opportunity in a changing global economy.

⚖️

Civil Rights & Equal Dignity

Scott has consistently supported policies rooted in fairness, dignity, and equal protection under the law, reflecting A4H's belief that all communities should be treated with respect and without discrimination.

🕉️

Respectful Engagement with Hindu & Indian American Communities

A4H appreciates leaders who engage sincerely and respectfully with Hindu American and Indian American communities while recognizing their growing contribution to America's civic, cultural, technological, and economic life.

🤝

Practical, Solutions-Oriented Governance

A4H values leaders who focus on solving real problems through thoughtful policy, constructive dialogue, and results-driven leadership rather than ideological division.

🗳️

Representation & Civic Participation

Scott's candidacy offers an opportunity for diverse communities, including Hindu Americans and Indian Americans, to continue building relationships with leaders who are accessible, engaged, and responsive to community priorities.
