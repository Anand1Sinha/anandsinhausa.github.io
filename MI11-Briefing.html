01 How This Plan Was Built

Built on four election cycles of Michigan SOS precinct-level data (2018–2024) covering all 299 precincts in Oakland County — the entirety of MI-11 post-2022 redistricting. Every number is traceable to source files. The analytical work produced a 10-sheet Excel workbook, a 163-page campaign playbook, and a targeting framework covering turnout, margins, swing trends, door-knock ROI, and a three-way race model.

07 Strengths & Opponent Dynamics
Dr. Kumar's Strengths
  • Elected to WSU Board of Governors (2018) — proven ballot performer
  • Physician credibility — healthcare is the top district issue
  • Indian American community — largest single ethnic bloc in MI-11
  • Independent positioning — captures DEM defectors + REP crossovers
  • Open seat — no incumbent advantage for anyone
  • Three-way race math — wins at 40%, not 50%
  • Dominant in Troy, West Bloomfield, Waterford — the three decisive cities
Opponent — Jeremy Moss (Projected DEM)
  • Strong institutional DEM backing — unions, party machinery
  • SD-7 State Senate base — name ID in parts of district
  • Superior early fundraising capacity
  • Weakness: Unknown in Troy, West Bloomfield, Waterford
  • Weakness: Progressive brand — wrong fit for suburban IND voters
  • Weakness: No Indian American community relationships

The central strategic insight: Moss will consolidate the Democratic base — and that is not enough to win MI-11 in a three-way race. The election is decided by 80,000–100,000 independent and soft-partisan suburban voters in Troy, Waterford, and West Bloomfield. Dr. Kumar's physician credibility, independent positioning, and Indian American community strength make him the natural plurality winner in exactly the geography that decides this race.
