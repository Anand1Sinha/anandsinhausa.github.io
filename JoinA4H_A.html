Still reading? Here is what is already happening — without our voice in the room:

Your child's future in admissions and on campus.

Indian American students are being passed over at elite universities despite outstanding records — treated as overrepresented. At Michigan, Harvard, and Columbia, mandatory diversity training frames Hindu practice as a social hierarchy to be dismantled. Your child is navigating a campus where their identity is a liability — written that way by people who never consulted us. In corporate America, DEI frameworks consistently disadvantage South Asians — the one high-achieving group these policies work against, not for.

Caste legislation — our traditions, someone else's definitions.

Hindu managers have been investigated for caste discrimination based on identity alone, before a single fact was established. Caste is being inserted as a legal category across multiple states — defined entirely by activist groups, with no input from our community.

  • Seattle has already passed a caste discrimination ordinance
  • California's SB403 was vetoed by Governor Newsom — and the movement immediately pledged to reintroduce it
  • Similar bills are active in Massachusetts, Colorado, and Minnesota

SB403 was not a grassroots uprising. It was a coordinated campaign run by Equality Labs — an organization that framed the entire bill around their own disputed caste survey. Their coalition included the Alphabet Workers Union, the Asian Law Caucus, TechEquity Collaborative, and the California Democratic Party. Equality Labs' documented funders include the Ford Foundation, the Nathan Cummings Foundation, the Omidyar Network, and the San Francisco Foundation — a well-resourced philanthropic network with a clear agenda.

Under these definitions, your temple associations, cultural gatherings, and family traditions become legally suspect. They have the infrastructure. We are building ours.

Our symbols, redefined without us.

Our sacred swastika — used in rangoli, temples, and weddings for 5,000 years — is being equated to the Nazi symbol in state legislation. Hindu Americans have been reported to authorities for displaying it in their own homes. These bills do not distinguish between a symbol of hatred and a symbol of blessing. Without our voice in the drafting room, they never will.

These are not isolated incidents. They are a coordinated pattern — moving state by state, institution by institution, with zero political consequence for anyone behind them.

The Evidence

Named Institutions. Documented Actions.

If you think these are distant or theoretical threats — they are not. The following institutions have already acted. These are documented policies and legislative records, not projections.

Implemented
Cisco Systems

A federal lawsuit revealed Cisco discriminated against a Dalit engineer — and internal HR policy treated caste as a protected category, effectively placing Hindu and Indian employees under a presumption of guilt. This is already in place at one of America's largest employers.

Implemented
Seattle, Washington — Caste Ordinance

Seattle became the first U.S. city to add caste as a protected category in anti-discrimination law. The ordinance was passed in 2023. More cities are watching. Detroit is not immune.

Vetoed — Will Return
California SB403

The bill that would have added caste discrimination to state law. Vetoed by Governor Newsom in 2023 after sustained advocacy — and its architects have stated publicly they will reintroduce it.

The organization behind SB403 is Equality Labs — the activist group that wrote the bill's framework, ran the statewide lobbying campaign, and produced the caste survey on which the entire legislative rationale rests. Equality Labs is funded through a network of left-progressive philanthropies including the Ford Foundation, the Omidyar Network, and Open Society-linked grant channels. Their full-time staff conducted over 700 advocacy meetings across California in a single legislative cycle. The lobbying infrastructure they built remains fully intact and active.

Active Campaign
Harvard University

Faculty and student groups have pushed to add caste to Harvard's non-discrimination policy. The campaign is ongoing. The framework being used is identical to what passed in Seattle.

Implemented
Brandeis University

Brandeis has added caste as a protected category in its official non-discrimination and harassment policies — making Hindu and Indian students subject to complaint under a framework they had no voice in shaping.

Implemented
Brown University

Brown has implemented caste protections. The standard used is based on activist frameworks that treat Hindu identity as inherently connected to caste oppression.

Active Campaign
University of Michigan — Ann Arbor

Advocacy groups have pushed U of M to adopt caste discrimination policies. This is our home state. This is personal.

Michigan is where A4H's chapter is most active. This is why local organization matters.

The Political Reality

We Are Bringing a Toothpick to a Gunfight

Every diaspora community that has achieved durable political influence in America did the same thing: it organized, it funded, and it showed up — year after year, election after election. The pattern is not a secret. The question is whether our community is willing to follow it.

Jewish American Political Infrastructure — AIPAC / United Democracy Project

AIPAC and its super PAC, the United Democracy Project, spent approximately $127 million combined in the 2023–24 election cycle — funding candidates, running ad campaigns, and actively opposing legislators they consider insufficiently aligned with their community's interests. This is the result of decades of sustained, organized community giving. It did not happen overnight.

Source: FEC filings, confirmed by OpenSecrets and Sludge investigative reporting. Figures cover the full 2023–24 federal election cycle.

Muslim American Political Infrastructure — Emgage

Emgage — the leading Muslim American political organization — is a useful benchmark for what organized community power looks like even at a fraction of AIPAC's scale. Their 2024 Federal PAC reported approximately $600,000 in direct candidate contributions. But that is just the candidate-check layer.

Their foundation and operational infrastructure — contributions exceeding $3.5 million in 2024 — funds full-time staff across seven states, 80+ political events per year, and a voter mobilization program they call "Million Muslim Votes." That is the machine that puts staff in the offices of Michigan and California legislators week after week, shaping policy before bills are ever written.

Verify it yourself: Emgage Federal PAC figures are public on FEC.gov. Foundation figures are disclosed in Emgage Foundation annual reports and IRS Form 990 filings, searchable at Candid/GuideStar. A4H FEC filings: FEC.gov → search "Americans4Hindus."

These communities are not our adversaries. They are simply organized — and we are not. Every dollar they raise, every legislator they meet, every bill they help shape is a reminder of what sustained political engagement produces. Hindu Americans are among the most educated and highest-earning communities in this country. The gap is not resources. It is organization.

While our community's professionals are building careers and companies, others are building the political infrastructure that shapes the laws governing those careers. Every community that has organized politically understands this — and acts accordingly. We have not. A4H is changing that.
