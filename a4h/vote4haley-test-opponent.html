Haley's Opponent in the Primary

A4H is not in the business of attacking candidates. But our community deserves to know the record — and the silence — of the other candidates in this race. This page presents documented, sourced facts. Readers can draw their own conclusions.

Public Record on Hindu American Issues

Abdul El-Sayed, the leading Democratic primary opponent to Haley Stevens, has run an extensive campaign for the United States Senate. A4H has reviewed his public record, platform, and statements for any engagement with Hindu American issues.

A4H's review of El-Sayed's campaign website, press releases, public statements, social-media accounts, and major media appearances as of July 2026 has not identified any public engagement with the specific Hindu American issues we track — including temple security, caste discrimination legislation, or outreach to Michigan's Hindu American community.

A4H believes voters should consider this absence when evaluating which candidate has demonstrated a commitment to representing Michigan's Hindu American community.

Haley Stevens made a different choice. See her documented record →

Statements on Record

ON ISRAEL'S RIGHT TO EXIST — MAY 2026

At a campaign event as reported by Jewish Insider (May 27, 2026), El-Sayed said he "struggles" with the question of whether Israel has a right to exist as a Jewish state. In A4H's reading of his remarks, he did not directly affirm that right and described certain Israeli policies as apartheid. Full statement: Jewish Insider →

Source: Jewish Insider, May 27, 2026 · CNN Interview, April 2026

A4H notes: A4H believes a candidate for the United States Senate should be able to clearly affirm the right of allied democracies to exist.

ON THE ISRAELI GOVERNMENT — CNN, APRIL 2026

El-Sayed told CNN Inside Politics (April 2026): "Hamas — evil. Israeli government — evil. We can say both." In A4H's reading of his remarks in the same interview, he characterized Israeli Prime Minister Netanyahu as a war criminal. Coverage: Jewish Insider →

Source: CNN Inside Politics, April 2026

A4H's Assessment

A4H does not question El-Sayed's right to hold his views or run for office. What A4H does assert — clearly and on the record — is the following:

A4H OPINION & ASSESSMENT

1. A4H has not identified any documented public record of engagement by El-Sayed with Hindu American issues, concerns, or community as of July 2026.

2. A4H has not identified any public statement by El-Sayed clarifying his position on caste discrimination legislation, which A4H believes harms our community.

3. His responses to attacks on houses of worship, and his positions on allied democracies, reflect a worldview that A4H believes is inconsistent with the values and security interests of the Hindu American community.

4. Haley Stevens has a documented, verifiable record of standing with our community. A4H's review of publicly available sources as of July 2026 has not identified a comparable documented record from El-Sayed.

5. In A4H's view, this difference matters — and it is why A4H has endorsed Haley Stevens.
