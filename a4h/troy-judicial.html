6th Circuit Court Judge

Vote for up to 2 candidates — top 2 advance to the November general election
Victoria King
14-year Oakland County Circuit Court staff attorney
Balanced / Center

Victoria King has spent 14 years as a judicial staff attorney inside Oakland County's 6th Circuit Court, advising sitting judges daily on civil, criminal, and family law matters. She is running for the seat being vacated by Judge Martha Anderson, who is term-limited. Her candidacy was encouraged by colleagues and judges she worked alongside for years.

She brings the deepest institutional knowledge of any candidate in this race — she knows the courtroom, the caseload, and the expectations from the inside. This translates to day-one competence and lower risk for constituents whose family, estate, or civil matters come before this court.

Notable endorsements: Former Michigan Supreme Court Chief Justice Robert Young Jr. (appointed by Republican Gov. Engler), sitting and retired Court of Appeals judges, multiple retired Circuit Court judges. No Democratic/progressive organization endorsements noted in public records.
A4H perspective: King's endorser base skews center to center-right, with no far-left organizational backing. Her 14-year track record within the court provides the most verifiable baseline of any candidate. Recommended consideration for Hindu American voters seeking judicial stability and balanced credentials.
Lisa Marie Neilson
Former Wayne County Probate Court judge — only candidate with prior bench experience
Center-Right

Lisa Marie Neilson is the only candidate in this race who has actually served as a judge. She was appointed to the Wayne County Probate Court in 2012 by Republican Governor Rick Snyder, giving her direct bench experience in a judicial setting. She is active in the Federalist Society (a center-right legal organization) and the Catholic Lawyers Society.

Her prior judicial appointment is a meaningful differentiator — she has already demonstrated the ability to manage a courtroom, write opinions, and handle the administrative responsibilities of a judgeship. For voters who value demonstrated judicial competence, she is the clearest choice on that dimension.

Affiliations: Federalist Society, Catholic Lawyers Society. Appointed by Republican Governor (2012). No progressive organizational endorsements found.
A4H perspective: Of the three candidates, Neilson has the most identifiable center-right ideological profile and the only proven judicial track record. For voters prioritizing "not far-left" and prior bench experience, she is the most clearly documented option.
Nadine Renee Hatten
Oakland County juvenile referee — repeat judicial candidate
Progressive-Leaning

Nadine Renee Hatten currently serves as a juvenile referee in Oakland County — a quasi-judicial role that handles juvenile court matters. She has run for judicial office previously. Her professional background is in juvenile and family law matters within the court system.

While her experience with juvenile proceedings is relevant, her endorsement profile signals more progressive organizational backing compared to King and Neilson. Judicial races rarely produce clear ideological declarations from candidates themselves, so endorser networks serve as the best available signal.

A4H perspective: Based on available endorsement signals, Hatten leans more progressive than the other two candidates. For Hindu American voters seeking balance or center-right judicial philosophy, King and Neilson have stronger credential profiles and clearer ideological alignment with community priorities.

Vote for 1 candidate — winner elected directly in November
Elisabeth Dery
Probate and elder-law attorney — deepest technical specialization
Balanced / Center

Elisabeth Dery has the deepest probate and elder-law practice of the three candidates. Probate court handles estates, wills, guardianships, conservatorships, and mental health commitments — all areas where Dery has built her legal practice. Her specialization directly matches the subject matter this court handles every day.

For Hindu American families navigating estate planning, guardianship for aging parents, or succession matters — the technical competence of a probate court judge is particularly consequential. Dery's focused practice in this area makes her the strongest technical candidate for this specific seat.

A4H perspective: Strongest subject-matter fit for this court. Probate matters (estates, guardianships, elder care) frequently affect Indian American families with complex cross-border asset structures. A technically qualified probate judge is a meaningful community interest.
