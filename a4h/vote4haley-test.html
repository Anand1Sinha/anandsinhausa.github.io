Americans4Hindus endorses Haley Stevens for U.S. Senate

For too long, Hindu American voices have been absent from the rooms where policy gets made. Haley Stevens is one of the rare elected officials who has worked to change that. Her opponents in this primary have not.

"She seats us at the table when others are putting us on the menu."

What our adversaries are doing to our community

This primary matters to every Hindu American — regardless of party. The threats below don't check your voter registration. They are active, documented, and expanding. This is why organized political engagement is not optional.

The Caste Trap — Bills positioning Hindus as oppressors

California SB 403 (2023) would have made any Hindu American employee liable for "caste discrimination" — with no offsetting benefits, only the risk of HR investigation. The bill was vetoed, but its architects have publicly stated they will reintroduce it.

This legislation is framed as a civil rights measure. It is not well-intentioned. It is a deliberate effort to brand the Hindu community as uniquely oppressive. The sponsoring organization ran over 700 advocacy meetings across California in a single legislative cycle. Their infrastructure is intact. Similar bills are moving in other states.

Meanwhile, Cisco, Brandeis, Brown, and multiple UC campuses have already embedded caste policies into HR and student conduct frameworks — no legislation required. Seattle became the first U.S. city to add caste as a protected category. The University of Michigan is under active pressure to follow. Your children carry this liability into every job application and campus they enter.

The Swastika — Our sacred symbol equated with hate

State legislators are moving bills that equate the Hindu swastika with the Nazi Hakenkreuz. Hindu Americans have already been reported to authorities for displaying it in home rangoli.

Our sacred swastika — used in temples, rangoli, and weddings for 5,000 years — has a different name, different origin, and different meaning from the Nazi symbol. Without our voice in the drafting room, these bills will never make that distinction.

Textbook battles — How our children are taught about us

California curriculum battles have sought to frame Hindu scripture and practice as inherently casteist in K–12 materials. Our history and faith are being written for us — by people who are not us.

The same activist frameworks behind SB 403 are driving the textbook campaign. What goes into those textbooks shapes how an entire generation of American children — including your own — understands Hinduism.

Temple and gurudwara vandalism — Attacks that go unanswered

Six Hindu temples in California were vandalized within two weeks. Rising hate crimes against Hindu Americans receive a fraction of the political attention given to other communities.

Without organized political relationships, these incidents don't reach the desk of a single elected official. No press conference. No condemnation. No follow-up. That is what political invisibility looks like in practice.

What she has done for Hindu Americans

Condemned Hinduphobia — on the record

She backed H.Res. 1131, a bipartisan House resolution explicitly condemning attacks on Hindu places of worship and anti-Hindu bigotry across the United States. It's in the congressional record.

Stood with Hindus facing persecution abroad

She supported H.Res. 1085, condemning terrorist attacks and religious persecution against Hindus in Afghanistan, and backing their resettlement as refugees in the United States — welcomed by the Hindu American Foundation as a milestone.

Caucus leadership on Dharmic community rights

She served as Vice Chair of the Sikh Congressional Caucus — relevant because Sikh and Hindu civil rights, temple security, and religious liberty concerns overlap directly. She has formal institutional experience engaging with Dharmic communities.

Committees that matter to Hindu American families

She sits on committees covering STEM policy, education, workforce development, and U.S.–China competition — exactly where Hindu American families' interests in immigration pipelines and high-skill careers get decided.

What Hindu Americans should do

We cannot afford to lose one of the few elected officials who has stood with us. What happens in Washington shows up in your life — in the HR policy your child faces at their first job, in the textbook that defines your faith to their classmates, in the ordinance that criminalizes the swastika on your front door, in the silence when your temple is vandalized and no one calls.

Haley Stevens has stood with our community. We need a voice where it counts — for you, your children, and your grandchildren.
