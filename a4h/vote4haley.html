Americans4Hindus endorses Haley Stevens for U.S. Senate

As America celebrates 250 years, Hindu Americans stand among those who helped build it — in our labs, our classrooms, our operating rooms, and our neighborhoods.

We have given freely, integrated deeply, and asked for little in return. That congenial spirit — rooted in millennia of civilization — is our greatest strength.

But strength without voice is invisible. And there are those who would use our silence, our sacred symbols, and our ancient traditions against us.

For too long, Hindu American voices have been absent from the rooms where policy gets made. Haley Stevens is one of the elected officials A4H believes has consistently worked to change that. A4H has not identified a comparable documented public record among her primary opponents.

Our voice will be heard. We have a seat at the table — and together, we can make a difference. Here is how →

"She seats us at the table when others are putting us on the menu."

What our adversaries are doing to our community

This primary matters to every Hindu American — regardless of party. These threats affect you whether you are Republican, Democrat, or independent. Your party registration is no protection. They are active, documented, and expanding. This is why organized political engagement is not optional.

The Caste Trap — Bills positioning Hindus as oppressors

A4H believes a bill was written that would make being Hindu a workplace liability. It was vetoed. They're bringing it back — in workplaces, cities, and universities.

A4H believes California SB 403 (2023), as written, would have exposed Hindu American employees to caste discrimination claims and HR review processes without offsetting protections. Its architects have publicly stated they intend to reintroduce it. This legislation is framed as a civil rights measure. A4H believes this legislation is not a well-intentioned civil rights measure. Rather, we believe it unfairly singles out Hindu Americans and risks stigmatizing the community. In A4H’s view, the bill reinforces the perception that Hindu Americans are uniquely associated with caste discrimination. The sponsoring organization conducted over 700 advocacy meetings across California during a single legislative cycle. Their advocacy infrastructure remains active, and similar legislative proposals are being introduced in other states.

Meanwhile, Cisco, Brandeis, Brown, and multiple UC campuses have already embedded caste policies into HR and student conduct frameworks — no legislation required. Seattle became the first U.S. city to add caste as a protected category. The University of Michigan is under active pressure to follow. Your children carry this liability into every job application and campus they enter.

The Swastika — Our sacred symbol equated with hate

Your rangoli can get you reported to the police. This is already happening.

Our sacred swastika — used in temples, rangoli, and weddings for 5,000 years — has a different name, different origin, and different meaning from the Nazi symbol. Without our voice in the drafting room, these bills will never make that distinction.

Textbook battles — How our children are taught about us

Someone is writing your children's understanding of Hinduism — and it isn't you. A4H believes what they're writing isn't accurate.

The same activist frameworks behind SB 403 are driving the textbook campaign. What goes into those textbooks shapes how an entire generation of American children — including your own — understands Hinduism.

Temple and gurudwara vandalism — Attacks that go unanswered

Six temples vandalized in two weeks. Zero press conferences. Any action taken to stop it? Here's why.

Without organized political relationships, these incidents don't reach the desk of a single elected official. No press conference. No condemnation. No follow-up. That is what political invisibility looks like in practice.

What she has done for Hindu Americans

Condemned Hinduphobia — on the record

She put anti-Hindu bigotry into the congressional record. Permanently.

She backed H.Res. 1131, a bipartisan House resolution explicitly condemning attacks on Hindu places of worship and anti-Hindu bigotry across the United States. It's in the congressional record.

Stood with Hindus facing persecution abroad

When Hindus were being killed in Afghanistan, one congresswoman sponsored a resolution.

She supported H.Res. 1085, condemning terrorist attacks and religious persecution against Hindus in Afghanistan, and backing their resettlement as refugees in the United States — welcomed by the Hindu American Foundation as a milestone.

Condemned the Pulwama terrorist attack — on record

In her first term, she said something about Kashmir that most American politicians won't say even today.

In February 2019, Haley Stevens issued a formal statement condemning the Pulwama suicide bombing that killed 40 Indian security personnel: "My prayers are with the victims of last week's horrendous terror attack in Kashmir. I stand with our friends in India to condemn this violence and stand united against terrorism." She said it when it mattered.

Caucus leadership on Dharmic community rights

She held a formal leadership role on the one caucus that deals with Dharmic community rights. A4H's review of publicly available sources as of July 2026 has not identified comparable caucus leadership among her primary opponents.

She served as Vice Chair of the Sikh Congressional Caucus — relevant because Sikh and Hindu civil rights, temple security, and religious liberty concerns overlap directly. She has formal institutional experience engaging with Dharmic communities.

Manufacturing, STEM, and the jobs our community depends on

Before Congress, she directed the Digital Manufacturing and Design Innovation Institute, which focused on strengthening domestic manufacturing capabilities. A4H believes her work there benefited Michigan's manufacturing base.

Hindu American engineers are concentrated in Michigan's auto and manufacturing corridor — Troy, Auburn Hills, Rochester Hills, and across Oakland County. The policy decisions made in these committees determine whether those jobs stay in Michigan, whether the supply chains they work in remain competitive, and whether their children inherit a manufacturing economy worth entering. She knows this sector from the inside. A4H's review of publicly available biographies as of July 2026 has not identified comparable manufacturing-sector experience among her primary opponents.
