The Ask

This is not a partisan ask. Michigan's primary is open — you receive one ballot with a Democratic column and a Republican column. You choose which column to vote in. No separate ballot is issued.

A4H is asking every Hindu American in Michigan to vote in the Democratic column on August 4 and choose Haley Stevens for U.S. Senate.

This is why this race matters — to every Hindu American

The politicians who write caste bills, staff the committees that govern our children's textbooks, and vote on ordinances that equate our sacred symbols with hate — they respond to organized communities with votes and relationships. We have neither, unless we build them.

Haley Stevens has demonstrated she will engage with our community. Her opponents in this primary have not. This is not a partisan ask — it is a community protection ask.

If you are a Hindu Republican in HD-56: vote in the Republican column for Ashok Baddi Ji.
If you are a Hindu Republican everywhere else: vote in the Democratic column for Haley Stevens — not because you're switching parties, but because you're protecting your community.

"She seats us at the table when others are putting us on the menu."

Politicians invest time and political capital where they see organized, consistent civic engagement. Haley Stevens has already demonstrated she will engage with our community. This vote is how we tell her — and every official watching — that the Hindu-American community notices, and rewards, that engagement.
