This Is Your Moment

Not another meeting. A turning point.

Hindu Americans are 5 million strong — among the most educated, most accomplished communities in this country. We are doctors, engineers, business owners, and community leaders. And yet, our political voice is nearly silent. Jewish Americans spend $17 per person on political action. Hindu Americans spend $0.02. That gap is why the Hinduphobia Protection Act has sat in Congress for years without passing.

That changes here. Behind you stands Americans4Hindus — a national organization with a defined mission, a proven model, and chapters building political power in states across the country. Michigan has the opportunity to be the most active, most effective chapter in the nation. We are not here to discuss. We are here to decide — and then do.

"We have the talent, the resources, and the civic potential. What we have been missing is the will to act together. That changes today. Michigan leads the way."

Why This Cannot Wait

The policy landscape is being built around us. Right now. Without us in the room.

Your children sit in classrooms where a state-approved textbook describes Hindu scriptures as "spells and charms." Their classmates read along. Your child has no language to defend what they know is sacred — because the textbook is the authority in that room, and the textbook has already decided.

This is not a distant threat. California SB-403 would have made being Hindu or Indian sufficient grounds for a workplace discrimination complaint — with no burden of proof, no offsetting benefit. It was vetoed. Its architects have publicly stated they will bring it back. The infrastructure — the staff, the funders, the 700+ lobbying meetings per legislative cycle — is fully intact. New York A6920 carries 33 co-sponsors and returns in January 2027. A Columbia Law Review proposal would extend this framework to every employer in the country under federal Title VII.

The pattern is consistent: a bill is introduced, vetoed, rebuilt, and reintroduced. Each cycle produces more co-sponsors and more refined language. Reversing a passed bill is ten times more difficult than stopping one. The window to act is before the vote — not after.

"Your safety was engineered by your parents' choices. It cannot be passed down like a home or a savings account. The systems your children will navigate — corporate HR departments, university conduct frameworks, state anti-discrimination law, school curricula — are being built right now. And they are being built largely without your participation."

— Anand Sinha, The Silent Shift, The Signal · June 2026

But there is proof the model works. Virginia HB-2783 (2025) explicitly protected the Hindu swastika while classifying the Nazi hakenkreuz as a Class 6 felony — because Hindu Americans organized, showed up, and had legislative relationships before the vote. That is the model. Michigan can lead it.
