Americans4Hindus Michigan Chapter · Membership Brief

Why This Moment Matters

If we are not at the table,
we will be on the menu.

Certain policies have been made — and more are coming — that directly affect Hindu Americans in schools, colleges, and workplaces. Most of us are not even aware this is happening.

Hindu Americans are among the most educated and most successful ethnic communities in the United States. We have built remarkable lives here. And yet on the one dimension that shapes the rules under which our children will grow up — political organization — we are far behind communities a fraction of our size.

This is not a complaint. It is a statement of fact that has a fix. Americans4Hindus (A4H) is that fix — the only Hindu-focused PAC in America, built to give our community a real seat at the table.

"When policies are written that affect our families, our faith, and our future — we will have a seat at the table. That is what A4H is building."

The Issues

What Is Already Happening to Our Community

These are not hypothetical threats. They are policies already on the books, bills that came within one vote of becoming law, and practices embedded in workplaces across America right now.

⚖️ The Caste Trap — SB403 & Corporate HR

California's SB403 would have made being Hindu or Indian enough to presume guilt of caste discrimination. It was vetoed after enormous pressure — and it will return. Meanwhile, major tech companies have already embedded caste clauses into HR policies. Your children will carry this into every job application and campus they enter.

🎓 DEI Frameworks & University Admissions

Indian American applicants are passed over at elite universities despite outstanding records — too "overrepresented" to benefit from DEI, not a protected category when it matters. No Hindu American organization had a seat at the table when these policies were written.

📚 Faith Misrepresented in Classrooms

Hinduism is consistently portrayed in K–12 curricula and media in ways that leave our children hesitant to acknowledge their faith openly. Other faith communities have the organized advocacy to shape how they are taught in schools. We do not. This is a direct consequence of our lack of political organization.

🕌 Temple Attacks & No Political Consequence

Rising hate crimes targeting Hindu Americans receive a fraction of the media and political attention given to similar attacks on other faith communities. Political attention follows political organization. We have neither.

🌐 No Rapid Response When Crisis Hits

Every crisis: 48 hours on WhatsApp, then silence. Zero political consequence for anyone. No pre-built infrastructure. No legislative relationships. The next crisis will not announce itself — and we will not be ready unless we build the machine now.

The Evidence

Named Institutions. Documented Actions.

If you think these are distant or theoretical threats — they are not. The following institutions have already acted. These are documented policies and legislative records, not projections.

Already Law or Policy
Cisco Systems

A federal lawsuit revealed Cisco discriminated against a Dalit engineer — and internal HR policy treated caste as a protected category, effectively placing Hindu and Indian employees under a presumption of guilt. This is already in place at one of America's largest employers.

Seattle, Washington — Caste Ordinance

Seattle became the first U.S. city to add caste as a protected category in anti-discrimination law. The ordinance was passed in 2023. More cities are watching. Detroit is not immune.

Brandeis University

Brandeis has added caste as a protected category in its official non-discrimination and harassment policies — making Hindu and Indian students subject to complaint under a framework they had no voice in shaping.

Brown University

Brown has implemented caste protections. The standard used is based on activist frameworks that treat Hindu identity as inherently connected to caste oppression.

Vetoed — Campaign Continues
California SB403

The bill that would have added caste discrimination to state law. Vetoed by Governor Newsom in 2023 after sustained advocacy — and its architects have stated publicly they will reintroduce it.

The organization behind SB403 is Equality Labs — the activist group that wrote the bill's framework, ran the statewide lobbying campaign, and produced the caste survey on which the entire legislative rationale rests. Equality Labs is funded through a network of left-progressive philanthropies including the Ford Foundation, the Omidyar Network, and Open Society-linked grant channels. Their full-time staff conducted over 700 advocacy meetings across California in a single legislative cycle. The lobbying infrastructure they built remains fully intact and active.

Active Campaigns
Harvard University

Faculty and student groups have pushed to add caste to Harvard's non-discrimination policy. The campaign is ongoing. The framework being used is identical to what passed in Seattle.

UC Davis & USC

Both campuses have active faculty and student campaigns to implement caste policies. The UC system-wide review is ongoing. Once one UC campus implements it, system-wide adoption typically follows.

University of Michigan — Ann Arbor

Advocacy groups have pushed U of M to adopt caste discrimination policies. This is our home state. This is personal.

Michigan is where A4H's chapter is most active. This is why local organization matters.

The Political Reality

We Are Bringing a Toothpick to a Gunfight

Every diaspora community that has achieved durable political influence in America did the same thing: it organized, it funded, and it showed up — year after year, election after election. Jewish Americans built AIPAC over decades. Cuban Americans built a network that has shaped US foreign policy for two generations. Irish Americans built machine politics that lasted a century. The pattern is not a secret. The question is whether our community is willing to follow it.

Jewish American Political Infrastructure — AIPAC / United Democracy Project

AIPAC and its super PAC, the United Democracy Project, spent approximately $127 million combined in the 2023–24 election cycle — funding candidates, running ad campaigns, and actively opposing legislators they consider insufficiently aligned with their community's interests. This is the result of decades of sustained, organized community giving. It did not happen overnight.

Source: FEC filings, confirmed by OpenSecrets and Sludge investigative reporting. Figures cover the full 2023–24 federal election cycle.

Muslim American Political Infrastructure — Emgage

Emgage — the leading Muslim American political organization — is a useful benchmark for what organized community power looks like even at a fraction of AIPAC's scale. Their 2024 Federal PAC reported approximately $600,000 in direct candidate contributions. But that is just the candidate-check layer.

Their foundation and operational infrastructure — contributions exceeding $3.5 million in 2024 — funds full-time staff across seven states, 80+ political events per year, and a voter mobilization program they call "Million Muslim Votes." That is the machine that puts staff in the offices of Michigan and California legislators week after week, shaping policy before bills are ever written.

Verify it yourself: Emgage Federal PAC figures are public on FEC.gov. Foundation figures are disclosed in Emgage Foundation annual reports and IRS Form 990 filings, searchable at Candid/GuideStar. A4H FEC filings: FEC.gov → search "Americans4Hindus."

These communities are not our adversaries. They are simply organized — and we are not. Every dollar they raise, every legislator they meet, every bill they help shape is a reminder of what sustained political engagement produces. Hindu Americans are among the most educated and highest-earning communities in this country. The gap is not resources. It is organization.

While our community's professionals are building careers and companies, others are building the political infrastructure that shapes the laws governing those careers. Every community that has organized politically understands this — and acts accordingly. We have not. A4H is changing that.

The Solution

What Americans4Hindus Is Doing

  • Supporting candidates across party lines who understand and respect Hindu Americans — the only PAC in America doing this specifically for our community
  • Working directly with the 26-member Hindu-Buddhist-Sikh-Jain Congressional Caucus to push back on Hinduphobic legislation before it becomes law
  • Building rapid-response infrastructure so the next crisis does not end in WhatsApp silence and zero political consequence
  • Training the next generation of Hindu American political leaders through the Hindu Rajneeti Academy — so 20 years from now, we are not still reacting from the outside
  • Expanding state chapter presence — including the active and growing Michigan chapter

The Hindu Rajneeti Academy is building the next generation of Hindu American political leaders. The organization is entirely volunteer-run with no salaries — all contributions go directly toward funding candidates who champion Hindu American issues.
