MI-11 Petition Signature Guide

Signature Quality Checklist

A quick guide for friends and neighbors who are willing to sign a ballot-access petition.

No vote commitment No donation No volunteer obligation

What does signing mean?

Signing simply helps a candidate qualify to appear on the ballot.

It does not mean you are endorsing the candidate, voting for the candidate, donating money, joining a campaign, or agreeing to receive campaign messages.

Please sign only if you are a registered voter in the district and the information you write matches your voter-registration address.

Before You Sign

  • Confirm you are a registered voter.
  • Confirm you live in the correct district / area covered by the petition.
  • Use your voter-registration address, not a mailing-only address.
  • Write clearly and completely so election officials can read your information.
  • Use the date you are actually signing.

How to Fill It Out Correctly

  • Signature: Sign as you normally sign official documents.
  • Printed name: Print clearly if the form asks for it.
  • Street address: Use your registered voting address.
  • City/Township: Write the city or township where you are registered.
  • Date: Use today’s date when you sign.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using a P.O. Box instead of your street address.
  • Leaving the address, city/township, or date blank.
  • Writing an old address if your voter registration has changed.
  • Using a date of birth instead of the signing date.
  • Signing if you are not registered to vote in the required district.

Quick FAQ

Am I promising to vote for this candidate?
No. Signing only helps place the candidate’s name on the ballot.
Am I donating or joining the campaign?
No. This is not a donation form, volunteer form, or mailing-list signup.
What happens if a signature is not valid?
Election officials may simply not count that signature. Campaigns collect extra signatures for this reason.
Can I sign if I am unsure whether I am registered in this district?
Please check first or skip signing to avoid an invalid signature.
CHECK
What if I have signed for another candidate — am I allowed to sign this too?
Michigan law says an individual shall not sign more nominating petitions for the same office than the number of people to be elected to that office. Since MI-11 elects one U.S. Representative, please sign only if you have not already signed another petition for a candidate running for U.S. Representative in MI-11.
