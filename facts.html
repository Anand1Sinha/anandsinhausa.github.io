The Corporate Rollout

Major Companies That Have Already Implemented Caste Policies

Several major corporations have added "caste" as a protected category in their HR anti-discrimination policies. The definitions used are broad enough to implicate Indian American employees in ways that no other ethnic group faces. Being Hindu, speaking an Indian language, or maintaining traditional cultural practices can now be cited as potential evidence of caste bias in a workplace complaint.

Corporate Implementations STATUS
Cisco Systems Implemented
The California Department of Fair Employment and Housing sued Cisco in 2020 for caste discrimination — the first such case in US legal history. Cisco subsequently added caste to its HR policies. The case set a precedent that has driven other companies to follow. Indian American employees are now uniquely exposed to caste-based complaints in ways no other ethnic group faces.
Apple, Google, IBM Under Pressure
Employee advocacy groups have formally petitioned multiple major tech companies to add caste to their anti-discrimination frameworks. Several have added internal guidelines. The pressure campaign is coordinated and ongoing. Without political pushback, full implementation across Silicon Valley is a matter of time, not if.
The Legislative Assault

Cities and States That Have Moved — And What Is Coming Next

Legislative Actions STATUS
Seattle, WA Law Passed
In February 2023, Seattle became the first US city to add caste as a protected category in its anti-discrimination ordinance. The law is now in effect. Hindu Americans living and working in Seattle are subject to caste-discrimination complaints under definitions that treat Indian cultural and religious practices as potential evidence of bias.
California SB403 Vetoed — Returning
SB403 passed both chambers of the California legislature in 2023 before being vetoed by Governor Newsom. The bill would have added caste to the state's anti-discrimination law under definitions that would have made temple associations, Sanskrit study, guru-shishya relationships, and Diwali gatherings legally suspect. The bill's sponsors have publicly stated their intention to reintroduce it. California's legislature will pass it again. The only question is whether the next governor signs it.
Colorado, Minnesota, Connecticut Bills Introduced
Similar caste discrimination bills have been introduced or are in active development in multiple other states. The legislative campaign is coordinated nationally. Without organized political opposition at the state level, these bills will pass one by one.

The Classroom Problem

How Our Faith Is Being Taught to Our Children

Separate from the caste issue, Hinduism is routinely misrepresented in K-12 education and university curricula in ways no other major faith tolerates. Ask any Hindu child in an American school what they have been taught about their faith. You will hear: Monkey God. 33 million Gods. The Shivling described in reductive, often crude terms. Our sacred swastika — used in Hindu Rangoli for 5,000 years — equated permanently with the Nazi Hakenkreuz.

Hindu Americans have been reported to authorities for displaying the swastika in Rangoli in their own homes. Our most auspicious symbol is effectively treated as a hate crime in parts of America. South Asian Studies programs at universities increasingly treat Hinduism not as a living spiritual tradition but as a social hierarchy to be dismantled. Hindu students on campuses face organized hostility with no political infrastructure behind them.

This is not a distant threat. It is happening in the schools your grandchildren attend and the universities your children graduated from.
