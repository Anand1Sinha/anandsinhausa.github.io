Community Briefing — 2026

Hindu Americans: most accomplished,
least politically organized.

Why that gap is no longer just embarrassing — and what we are doing about it.

AS
Anand Sinha
Director, Outreach — A4H Michigan Chapter

We are the most educated, highest-earning ethnic group in America. And one of the least politically organized. That gap is no longer just embarrassing. It is costly — and the costs are falling on our children.

Every other community of comparable standing figured out long ago that influence in the decisions that shape your life requires organized political presence. Jewish Americans built AIPAC. Cuban Americans built powerful networks in Florida. Irish Americans shaped machine politics for a century. We have been largely absent. It is time that changed.

When policies are written that affect our families, our faith, and our future — we will have a seat at the table. That is what Americans4Hindus is building.

"If we are not at the table, we will be on the menu."

What is at stake

Five Issues Directly Affecting Our Community

1. The Caste Trap — A Legal Weapon Targeting Hindu Identity

Being Hindu or of Indian origin is increasingly enough to be presumed guilty of caste discrimination. This is already embedded in major tech company HR policies and university non-discrimination frameworks. Your children will carry this into every job application, every campus, every workplace.

2. Hinduism Misrepresented in Classrooms and on Campuses

Ask any Hindu child what they have been taught about their faith. Monkey God. 33 million Gods. The Shivling misrepresented. Our sacred swastika — used in Rangoli for 5,000 years — equated to the Nazi Hakenkreuz. Hindu Americans have been reported to authorities for displaying it in their own homes. South Asian Studies programs treat Hinduism not as a living spiritual tradition but as a social hierarchy to be dismantled.

3. DEI Frameworks Working Against Our Children

Indian American students are being passed over at elite universities despite outstanding records — treated as "overrepresented." In corporate environments, South Asians are the one group DEI initiatives consistently disadvantage. Without an organized political voice, we have no seat at the table when these policies are written.

4. Temple Attacks Met With Political Silence

Hate crimes targeting Hindu Americans have risen steadily and receive a fraction of the media and political attention given to similar attacks on other communities. Political attention follows political organization. We have neither.

5. No Rapid Response When Crisis Hits

Every time a hostile bill surfaces or a temple is vandalized, we trend on WhatsApp for 48 hours and disappear — with zero political consequence for anyone. We have no pre-built infrastructure, no legislative relationships, no rapid response. The next crisis will not announce itself.

If you think these are distant or theoretical threats — they are not. The following institutions have already acted. These are not warnings. They are facts on record, documented and in effect today.

The documented record

Named Institutions That Have Already Moved Against Hindu American Interests

Each institution below took action against our community because there was no organized political consequence for doing so. That is precisely what A4H exists to change.

Corporate Sector STATUS
Cisco Systems Implemented
The California Department of Fair Employment and Housing sued Cisco in 2020 for caste discrimination — the first such case in US legal history. Cisco subsequently added caste to its HR policies. The case set a precedent driving other companies to follow. Indian American employees are now uniquely exposed to caste-based complaints in ways no other ethnic group faces.
Apple, Google, IBM Under Pressure
Employee advocacy groups have formally petitioned multiple major tech companies to add caste to their anti-discrimination frameworks. Several have added internal guidelines. The campaign is coordinated and ongoing. Without political pushback, full implementation across Silicon Valley is a matter of when, not if.
Legislative Actions STATUS
Seattle, WA Law in Effect
In February 2023, Seattle became the first US city to add caste as a protected category in its anti-discrimination ordinance. The law is now in effect. Hindu Americans living and working in Seattle are subject to caste-discrimination complaints under definitions that treat Indian cultural and religious practices as potential evidence of bias.
California SB403 Vetoed — Returning
SB403 passed both chambers of the California legislature in 2023 before being vetoed by Governor Newsom. The bill would have made temple associations, Sanskrit study, guru-shishya relationships, and Diwali gatherings legally suspect. The bill's sponsors have publicly stated their intention to reintroduce it. The only question is whether the next governor signs it.
Colorado, Minnesota, Connecticut Bills Introduced
Similar caste discrimination bills have been introduced or are in active development in multiple other states. The legislative campaign is nationally coordinated. Without organized political opposition at the state level, these bills will pass one by one.
Universities STATUS
Harvard University Implemented
Added caste to its non-discrimination policy. Hindu students and faculty are subject to caste-discrimination complaints under broad definitions that conflate cultural and religious practice with bias.
Brandeis University Implemented
Added caste to its non-discrimination and anti-harassment policies.
Brown University Implemented
Added caste to its non-discrimination policy following student advocacy campaigns.
UC Davis Implemented
Added caste protections to its anti-discrimination framework.
USC — University of Southern California Active Campaign
Active campaign by student groups to add caste to USC's non-discrimination policy. Hindu student organizations have raised objections. Outcome pending. Without organized political infrastructure backing those objections, the campaign will succeed.
University of Michigan Active Campaign
Organized effort underway to add caste to U of M's non-discrimination framework. Hindu American alumni and community groups have pushed back. Without political infrastructure at the state level — including relationships with Michigan legislators — that pushback has limited leverage. This is our home state. This is personal.
The Solution

Americans4Hindus — The Only Hindu PAC in America

Every other Hindu organization in America is a 501(c)(3) advocacy group — legally prohibited from working in the political space. Americans4Hindus (A4H) is different. It is the only registered Hindu Super PAC in America — the one organization legally able to fund candidates, build relationships with legislators, and fight back politically before the next bill passes, not after.

All-volunteer. No salaries. Funds audited and reported to federal and state agencies every three months — results published publicly. When we are not engaged in public policy, important decisions get made without fully understanding our perspective. A4H exists to change that.
